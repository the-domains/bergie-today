Around Alone

On December 24, 1968, astronaut Bill Anders, lunar module pilot on the Apollo 8 mission-his first and only space flight-took a photograph. In it, the distant blue earth, half in shadow, rises above the gray curve of the moon’s surface. Anders and the other crew members, Frank Borman and Jim Lovell, were the first humans to orbit the moon. They were also the first to travel far away enough from our planet that they could look back and see it whole, a blue sphere against a void.
